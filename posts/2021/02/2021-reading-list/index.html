2021 Reading List

I noticed that I have been reading books a lot less – and scrolling social media a lot more – so one of my New Year’s resolutions was to read as much as possible. Here’s what I have lined up so far:

Career

  • Designing Data Intensive Applications by Martin Kleppmann
  • Zero to One by Peter Thiel
  • Never Split the Difference by Chris Voss
  • Atomic Habits by James Clear
  • Principles of Computer System Design: An Introduction by Jerome H. Saltzer
  • Clean Architecture: A Craftsman’s Guide to Software Structure and Design by Robert Martin
  • Big Data: Principles and Best Practices of Scalable Real-Time Data Systems by Nathan Marz

Non-Fiction

  • Genghis Khan and the Making of the Modern World by Jack Weatherford
  • The Deficit Myth: Modern Monetary Theory and the Birth of the People’s Economy by Stephanie Kelton
  • The Divide: A Brief Guide to Global Inequality and its Solutions by Jason Hickel
  • Capital in the 21st Century by Thomas Piketty
  • The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham
  • The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich by William L. Shirer
  • An Introduction to the Sociology of Religion by Inger Furseth
  • An Introduciton to Quantum Computing by Phillip Kaye
  • The Selfish Gene by Richard Dawkins
  • Guns, Germs, and Steel by Jared Diamond
  • Why We Sleep: Unlocking the Power of Sleep and Dreams by Matthew Walker
  • How to Change your Mind by Michael Pollan

Fiction

  • Tuesdays with Morrie: An Old Man, A Young Man and Life’s Greatest Lesson by Mitch Albom
  • Never Let Me Go by Kazuo Ishiguro
  • Recursion by Blake Crouch
  • Dune by Frank Herbert
  • The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini
  • On Earth We’re Briefly Gorgeous by Ocean Vuong
